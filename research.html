Finding 01 3%

Signal Hit Rate After Significance Testing

368 signals tested. Only 11 pass p<0.05 — a 3% hit rate consistent with random selection under multiple-comparison correction. Best individual signals: ADX_14 (lift +4.4%, p=0.015), gap_pct (lift +3.8%, p=0.024).

Finding 02 −0.52

Causal Filters Hurt Sharpe

VIX+causal Sharpe is −0.52 versus VIX+MOM20 at +1.08. Causal relationships exist (SPY Granger-causes all Indian sectors, p<1e−20) but are already embedded in the VIX signal — adding them creates noise, not edge.

Finding 03 T+1

Tuesday Close Beats Thursday Close

T+1 exit (Tuesday close) consistently outperforms T+3 (Thursday close) across all signal combinations and periods tested. Confirmed robust across regime changes — applies to ADX33 variant as well as legacy signal sets.

Methodology

01 — Regime Classification

BIC-Optimal GMM

Market regimes identified via walk-forward GMM (k=4–7 selected by BIC) on 7 features: VIX level, VIX trend, Nifty momentum, rate acceleration, vol ratio, IT/bank sector dispersion, and SPX correlation. Regime-conditional strategy routing replaces one-size-fits-all signals.

02 — Signal Validation

DSR + Per-Regime Testing

Each signal tested via Deflated Sharpe Ratio (adjusts for number of strategies trialled) and Mann-Whitney U test per regime. Only signals with DSR > 0 and p<0.05 per relevant regime are retained. VIX_GATE is the only strategy passing all tests.

03 — OOS Protocol

Strict Walk-Forward

All results on strictly out-of-sample data. Expanding window refit with 21-day purge gap. Pre-2022 / post-2022 split as natural regime boundary. Bootstrap CI and PSR computed. Overfit audit maps each component to its valid regime and IS vs OOS Sharpe.
